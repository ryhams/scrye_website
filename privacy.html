Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 16, 2026

About This Policy

Scrye is an iOS app that helps you understand and manage your screen time. This policy describes how the current version of Scrye handles your data.

What Scrye Accesses

Screen time and app usage data — Scrye uses Apple's DeviceActivity framework (part of Screen Time) to read your device usage. This access is granted through iOS's Family Controls permission, which you must explicitly approve. In the current version of the app, all processing happens on-device and this data is not transmitted externally.

App settings and preferences — Your app settings, limits, and preferences are saved locally on your device using iOS standard storage.

Data Collection

The current version of Scrye does not require an account and does not collect personally identifiable information such as your name, email address, location, or contacts. There are no third-party analytics, advertising networks, or tracking SDKs in the app.

Third Parties

Scrye currently does not share data with third-party services. The app relies on Apple's Screen Time API, which operates within iOS and is subject to Apple's own privacy policies.

Children

The current version of Scrye does not collect personal information from users of any age. If future versions introduce family features, any data involved would be used solely to enable those features — it would not be examined, sold, or shared with third parties. This policy will be updated to reflect any such changes before they are released.

Policy Updates

This policy may be updated as the app evolves. When changes are made, the date above will be updated. Material changes will be noted in the App Store release notes. Continued use of the app following an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Governing Law
